Foreshadowing

Now here is the $1 Million question. The future. What will I do? For a more short-term goal, I want to be able to complete all of my classes passing with at least a 90. This is extremely important because my long-term goal requires exemplary grades and SAT scores. Along with these requirements, I need numerous extracurriculars so that I can prove the person I am beyond my test scores and grades. I want to be able to demonstrate that I am the perfect candidate.

For that long-term goal I was talking about, I want to be able to ensure admission to the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). I have been researching colleges for a while now and it seems to me that MIT is the best choice for me. It has all of the classes and fields I want to pursue and it has been recommended to me by many, many people. It is a great match for me, but it will not be easy. The acceptance rate is 4%. That means 4/100 people who apply get accepted. The acceptance requirements for MIT are as follows:

  • Average GPA: 4.19
  • Average SAT Scores:
    • Math: 791
    • Reading + Writing: 752
    • Composite: 154.3
  • Average ACT Score: 35

These are extremely hard goals to reach so that is why my short-term goal is what it is. With these goals motivating me I hope I can get accepted into the prestigious MIT.
