Setting

Where does this story (in my case, life) happen? Where is it located? Why right here of course. I was born and raised here, in Sugar Land, Texas. I started off my life in an apartment and moved to two others before ending up in the lovely home I live in to this day. It is also where I made most of my most important memories. We are a family of 4, including me, my sister (Iyra), my mom (Parveen), and my dad (Imran). My parents get a room, I get a room, my sister gets a room, and and we have one guest bedroom. Now, where did my parents come from? Well, my parents were both born and raised in Pakistan. From there they moved here to the US. According to them, it was not easy. They had to go through not 1, not 2, but 4 applications to earn their citizenship and be legal United States citizens. I cannot even begin to fathom how much of a pain that must have been for them.

One room in my house I want to talk about is the Office. Here is where I pulled all-nighters, worked all day, retaught myself lessons, etc. Mostly because of my procrastination but that is beside the point. What I am trying to say is that I have completed so many projects, so many assignments, and so many test reviews that this room has become one of my two sacred spaces in the house. In fact, I am writing this, right now in the Office. Yeah, sure it is associated with 3+ years of trauma, and I have a little bit of PTSD from the scoldings for not completing my homework or for late assignments, but it still is very near and dear to my heart, and for that reason I felt it was required to insert this paragraph.

Really quick, notice how I mentioned that the Office was one of two sacred spaces in the house. The other one has to be my room. Yeah, yeah I know. I can already hear you all saying "Um, obviously your room is one. Is it not everyone's? ‚òùÔ∏èü§ì" And to that I say, no. For me, my room was not a place where I could hang out any time. This was the case after the 8th grade. Before that, I would only go to my room to sleep, charge my phone, and pull all-nighters binge-watching Netflix. It was not that I was not allowed to go in, I just did not. I usually just stayed downstairs talking to my family, watching TV, or something to pass the time. Now, my room (specifically my bed) is a spot that I can go to, to rid my mind of worries, relax after learning all day, and yes, binge-watch Netflix.

So you can see that my house is the place where I feel safe and where most of my life occurred. I was not a very busy kid, so I picked up some hobbies. The most significant (and the only one I actually stuck to) was programming. I programmed my first website at the age of nine, and I LOVED it. I could not stop learning about it. That is how we got here. More about this later.
